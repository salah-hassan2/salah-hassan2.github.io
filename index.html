About Me

I am a PhD student in the Department of Physics at Northeastern University, specializing in biophysics and computational biology. My research focuses on understanding the molecular mechanisms of viral fusion through advanced computational simulations.

Research

HIV Envelope Protein Dynamics

My primary research investigates the conformational changes of the HIV envelope (Env) protein during the viral fusion process. Using large-scale molecular dynamics (MD) simulations, I study how the gp160 protein undergoes structural rearrangements that enable the virus to enter host cells.

Research Interests

• Viral fusion mechanisms and membrane dynamics

• Conformational changes in envelope glycoproteins (gp120/gp41)

• Six-helix bundle formation and fusion peptide insertion

• Structure-function relationships in viral entry

• Computational biophysics and biomolecular simulations

Computational Approaches

I employ molecular dynamics simulations to capture atomic-level details of protein conformational transitions. My work involves analyzing hundreds to thousands of simulation trajectories to understand the coupling between different structural domains and identify key intermediates in the fusion pathway.
